# What Is Activated Carbon

Activated carbon is produced from natural materials such as **coconut shells, peat, hardwood, softwood, lignite coal, bituminous coal, olive pits, and other specialty materials**.

Activated Carbon, also known as **Activated Charcoal (HS CODE: 38021000, CAS: 7440-44-0)**, is widely recognized as a **broad-spectrum adsorbent** used in numerous industrial and environmental applications.

Due to its **high porosity, extensive surface area, and strong surface reactivity**, activated carbon is extremely effective in:

- Separation and purification processes  
- Catalytic reactions  
- Biomedical applications  
- Environmental protection and pollution control  

Its remarkable adsorption capability makes it an ideal solution for **removing impurities and contaminants from liquids and gases**.

---

# Properties of Activated Carbon

Activated carbon is characterized by:

- Extremely high porosity  
- Surface area ranging from **500 to 3000 m²/g**  
- Highly developed internal pore structure  
- Diverse surface chemistry  

These properties enable activated carbon to effectively:

- Adsorb pollutants  
- Remove impurities from liquids and gases  
- Improve filtration systems  
- Support separation and solvent recovery processes

# Coconut Shell Activated Carbon

Coconut shell activated carbon is produced from **coconut husk using a vapor activation process**. Since it contains no chemical additives, it does not react with or contaminate the medium in which it is used.

This product is **environmentally friendly, naturally sourced, and has a low carbon footprint**. Its micro-pore molecular structure allows it to effectively absorb small contaminants, making it ideal for **drinking water purification**.

Due to its **high abrasion resistance and excellent physical and chemical properties**, coconut shell activated carbon is widely used in applications such as:

- Drinking water purification  
- Air and gas purification  
- Gold and precious metal recovery  
- Industrial filtration processes  

We are proud to be one of the **leading Coconut Shell Activated Carbon manufacturers in India**, supplying high-performance carbon for water, air, gas, and metal recovery applications.

# Coal Activated Carbon

Coal-based activated carbon is manufactured from various coal types, including:

- Bituminous coal  
- Anthracite  
- Lignite  

Among these, **bituminous coal-based activated carbon** is widely preferred due to its higher micro-porosity and stronger mechanical strength.

Although coal activated carbon may have slightly lower adsorption capacity compared to coconut shell or wood-based carbon, it performs effectively in **high-concentration applications** and offers advantages such as **lower cost and greater availability**.

Applications include:

- Industrial gas purification  
- Drinking water treatment  
- Pond water purification  
- Wastewater treatment  

Our coal activated carbon is manufactured with **low impurity levels and reduced ash content** to ensure consistent performance.

# Water Washed Activated Carbon

Water washing is a process used to **remove excess ash content from granular activated carbon**, improving its purity and performance.

This process can also be used during the **regeneration of activated carbon**, restoring its adsorption efficiency.

We provide **water-washed activated carbon in various mesh sizes**, customized according to customer requirements.

# Acid Washed Activated Carbon

Acid washing is performed to **remove mineral impurities and reduce ash content** in activated carbon.

During this process, acids dissolve minerals such as:

- Magnesium  
- Silica  
- Iron  
- Nickel  
- Copper  

This results in a **high-purity activated carbon** with superior adsorption characteristics.

### Key Features

- Extensive internal pore structure  
- Neutral surface chemistry  
- Maximum hardness and durability  
- Long operational life  
- High activity and rapid pH stabilization  

### Applications

Acid washed activated carbon is commonly used in:

- Pharmaceutical purification processes  
- Food and beverage production  
- High-purity water treatment plants  
- Condensate de-oiling systems  
- Double layer capacitors  
- Protection of reverse osmosis membranes from chlorine and organic fouling

# Wood Activated Carbon

Wood-based activated carbon is produced from **carefully selected wood sources** through controlled carbonization and activation processes.

This type of carbon offers **high purity and a well-developed pore structure**, making it particularly suitable for:

- Water filtration systems  
- Food and beverage purification  
- Deodorization processes  
- Pharmaceutical and chemical filtration systems  

Wood activated carbon features **large pore volumes and high surface area**, which improves filtration efficiency and reduces processing time.

While it may not remove very fine contaminants as effectively as micro-pore carbons, its **meso- and macro-pore structure** makes it ideal for certain specialized filtration applications.

Additionally, since it is derived from **renewable sources**, wood activated carbon has a **lower environmental impact**.
