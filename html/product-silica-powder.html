Product Overview

Silica Powder is a finely ground form of silicon dioxide (SiO₂) derived from high-purity quartz. It appears as a fine white powder and is widely used across industries such as construction, glass manufacturing, electronics, ceramics, paints, and cosmetics.

Due to its high purity, excellent thermal stability, and chemical inertness, silica powder is an essential raw material for many industrial processes. It provides enhanced durability, improved mechanical strength, and superior resistance to heat and chemicals in numerous applications.

Our silica powder is manufactured with controlled particle size and high purity levels to ensure consistent quality and reliable industrial performance.

Key Features

  • High purity silicon dioxide content (99–99.5%)
  • Fine particle size distribution
  • Excellent thermal stability
  • High hardness and abrasion resistance (Mohs Hardness: 7)
  • Chemically inert and non-reactive
  • Consistent whiteness (90–98%) and particle uniformity
  • Suitable for multiple industrial applications

Applications

Silica powder is widely used across a range of industries due to its high strength, chemical stability, and thermal resistance.

Glass Industry

Primary raw material for manufacturing flat glass, container glass, optical glass, and specialty glass products.

Electronics Industry

Used in semiconductor manufacturing, solar panel production, and electrical insulation materials.

Ceramics Industry

Enhances strength and durability in ceramic tiles, sanitary ware, and heat-resistant ceramic products.

Construction Industry

Improves concrete performance by increasing compressive strength by 15–25%, reducing water permeability, and enhancing durability of structures.

Paints and Coatings Industry

Functions as an additive to improve scratch resistance, UV protection, thermal resistance, and surface durability.

Cosmetics Industry

Used as anti-caking agent, flow improver, and absorbent material in cosmetic formulations.

Role in Construction

Silica powder plays a critical role in high-performance concrete applications. When added to cement mixtures, it reacts with calcium hydroxide produced during cement hydration. This pozzolanic reaction forms additional calcium silicate hydrate (C-S-H), which fills microscopic voids in the concrete structure.

Benefits in Concrete:
  • 15–25% increase in compressive strength
  • Reduced water permeability
  • Enhanced durability in harsh environments
  • Increased resistance to chloride attack
  • Improved lifespan of structures by 20–30+ years
Common Construction Applications:
  • Bridges and tunnels
  • High-rise buildings
  • Marine structures
  • Precast concrete components
  • Industrial flooring systems
