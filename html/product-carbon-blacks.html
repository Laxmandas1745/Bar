Product Overview

Carbon black is an engineered form of elemental carbon used as a reinforcing filler, pigment, and performance additive across multiple industries. It consists of extremely fine carbon particles that fuse into complex three-dimensional aggregates, giving it unique mechanical, electrical, and optical properties.

It is produced through controlled partial combustion or pyrolysis of hydrocarbons such as heavy petroleum oils, refined coal tar, or natural gas at very high temperatures. The resulting fine black powder delivers reinforcing strength, UV protection, and conductivity benefits that improve durability and performance in demanding applications.

What is Carbon Black?

Carbon black refers to engineered carbon nanoparticles that form fused three-dimensional aggregates. It appears as a fine black powder and is produced through partial combustion and pyrolysis of hydrocarbon feedstocks under controlled high-temperature conditions.

Carbon Black vs Charcoal: Carbon black is produced from hydrocarbon feedstocks in gas-phase processes, while charcoal is derived from biomass pyrolysis at lower temperatures and has a coarse bulk structure. Their performance and applications are fundamentally different.

Feedstock Used

  • Heavy petroleum oils
  • Refined coal tar oils
  • Natural gas

Sustainable alternatives include recycled tire-derived oils and biomass-derived hydrocarbons.

General Applications

  • Tire reinforcement and automotive rubber components
  • Plastics, coatings, and printing inks
  • Sealants, adhesives, and electronic components
  • UV protection, color strength, and electrical conductivity

Storage and Shelf Life

  • Store in a dry, cool, well-ventilated environment
  • Protect from moisture and high humidity

Per ASTM D8043, carbon black can have an indefinite shelf life. Moisture content and iodine number may change slowly but generally do not affect rubber performance.

Handling Guidelines

  • Avoid dust formation and inhalation
  • Provide adequate local exhaust ventilation
  • Do not use compressed air to clean dust deposits
  • Prevent static discharge and ignition sources
  • Use PPE such as respirators, goggles, gloves, and protective clothing

Recycling and Sustainability

Recovered carbon black from post-consumer products such as used tires can be processed for reuse in tires, rubber products, and industrial materials—supporting circular economy initiatives.

Quality Evaluation

Quality is typically assessed through a Certificate of Analysis (COA) with parameters such as:

  • Surface area (iodine number or nitrogen adsorption)
  • Structure (Oil Absorption Number – OAN)
  • Purity and cleanliness (sieve residue)
  • Moisture content
